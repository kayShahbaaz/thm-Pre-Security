- TLD (Top-Level Domain)
    -  [tryhackme.com](http://tryhackme.com) TLD is .com
    - two types of TLD, gTLD (Generic Top Level) and ccTLD (Country Code Top Level Domain)
    - gTLD was meant to tell the user the domain name's purpose; for example, a .com would be for commercial purposes, .org for an organisation, .edu for education and .gov for government.
    - And a ccTLD was used for geographical purposes, for example, .ca for sites based in Canada, .co.uk for sites based in the United Kingdom and so on. 
- Second Level Domain:
    -  [tryhackme.com](http://tryhackme.com) as an example, the .com part is the TLD, and tryhackme is the Second Level Domain
    - When registering a domain name, the second-level domain is limited to 63 characters + the TLD and can only use a-z 0-9 and hyphens (cannot start or end with hyphens or have consecutive hyphens).
- Subdomain

DNS Record types:

- multiple types of DNS record exist.
- A Record, resolve to IPv4 addresses
- AAAA Record, resolve to IPv6 addresses
- CNAME Record, resolves subdomains
- MX Record, handle the email for the domain you are querying
- TXT Record
    - text-based data
    - list servers that have the authority to send an email on behalf of the domain, help in the battle against spam and spoofed email
    - used to verify ownership of the domain name when signing up for third party services.
